About
Alexey Listopad worked in painting, scenography, and film, developing a visual language shaped by Baltic light, structure, and atmosphere.
Born in 1950, he spent his formative years in Klaipeda and later studied at the Penza Art College named after K.A. Savitsky. His career moved between painting, exhibition practice, and film design, with works now held in museum and private collections across Europe, the United States, and Asia.

Collections

Works by Alexey Listopad are held in museum and private collections in Russia, Lithuania, Poland, France, Germany, the United States, England, China, and Australia.
Exhibitions
The work has been presented in institutional and gallery contexts across Moscow, St. Petersburg, Lithuania, and other European locations.
