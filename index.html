About

A painter between discipline, atmosphere, and memory

Alexey Listopad (1950–2024) developed a painterly language shaped by Baltic light, academic structure, and a quiet scenographic intelligence. Born in the north and formed in Klaipėda, he carried into maturity a visual sensitivity to shoreline weather, industrial edges, and tonal restraint.

Alongside painting, he worked within cinematic and scenographic environments. That experience informed the spatial intelligence of the later canvases: composed rather than accidental, luminous rather than decorative, inward rather than descriptive.

The paintings do not insist; they unfold gradually. Their atmosphere is held in suspension, as though memory itself were still settling into light.

Estate

A controlled archive, not an open inventory

The estate was established to preserve, document, and gradually present the work following the artist’s passing. The archive consists of approximately 300 paintings spanning several decades of practice.

Selected works are presented publicly. A broader group remains available upon request for collectors, advisors, and institutions. This preserves hierarchy, avoids oversupply, and supports a collector-grade market position.

The visual rhythm remains quiet, but the sales structure becomes more disciplined: curated release, private inquiry, controlled inventory.
