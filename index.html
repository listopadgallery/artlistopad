About the Artist

Alexey Evgenievich Listopad (1950–2025) was born in Nyandoma, Arkhangelsk region, and spent his childhood and youth in Klaipėda on the Baltic Sea, where he also completed art school. From 1970 to 1974 he studied at the Penza Art School named after K. A. Savitsky. Between 1975 and 1979 he taught in Klaipėda and participated in exhibitions of the Lithuanian SSR.

He moved to Moscow in 1980, joined the youth association of the Moscow Union of Artists in 1981, and worked in cinema as a production designer from 1989 to 1994. In 2004 he relocated to Dolgoprudny; since 2005 he had been a member of the Union of Artists of Russia. His mature language joined figurative painting and landscape with theatrical structure, tonal nuance, and inward atmosphere.

“The canvases live, breathe, and shimmer; they always contain a certain mystery and expectation — an image of the multidimensionality of the world. The works are spiritualized, filled with inner light and energy. They are metaphysical and do not reveal themselves immediately.”Dolgoprudny Historical and Art Museum, announcement for the 2021 solo exhibition “Mirages of the World”

Exhibitions & Collections

A concise institutional profile drawn from the Dolgoprudny museum exhibition page and used here to strengthen collector confidence.

CollectionsWorks are held in state museums and private collections in Russia, Lithuania, Poland, France, Germany, the USA, England, China, and Australia.
MediumMost works are executed in oil painting and mixed media; the estate’s core sales inventory is primarily oil on canvas.
FocusThe artist gave preference to figurative painting and landscape, especially Baltic and maritime motifs.
Institutional presenceThe artist exhibited in Moscow, Saint Petersburg, Dolgoprudny, Klaipėda, and Nida, with both group and solo exhibitions.

Selected exhibition timeline

  • 1988 — All-Union Youth Exhibition, Manege, Moscow.
  • 1990 — Solo exhibition at the House of Cinema, Moscow (sketches and paintings for the film “Sphinx”).
  • 1992 — Solo exhibition at the House of Cinema, Moscow (for the film “Presence”).
  • 2003–2007 — Exhibitions in the Central House of Artists and associated galleries in Moscow.
  • 2007 — Solo exhibition at the Dolgoprudny Historical and Art Museum.
  • 2010 — Solo exhibition in Nida, Lithuania; anniversary exhibition with the Union of Artists of Russia.
  • 2015 — Solo exhibition at the Union of Artists of Russia.

  • 2020 — Solo exhibition “Eternal Themes”, Dolgoprudny Historical and Art Museum.
  • 2021 — Solo exhibition “Mirages of the World”, Dolgoprudny Historical and Art Museum.

The Estate

Following the artist’s passing, the estate presents a curated body of signed paintings with the aim of building a stable long-term collector market rather than encouraging rapid dispersal. The combination of institutional exhibition history, cross-border private collections, and a coherent Baltic-centered visual language gives the archive strong positioning for thoughtful placement in Europe.
