About
A painter shaped by Baltic light, disciplined structure, and a sustained attention to atmosphere.

Alexey Listopad developed a visual language in which compositional discipline and atmospheric reserve remain inseparable. Coastal light, muted architecture, labor, water, and silence form the recurring emotional field of the work.

In the mature paintings, narrative recedes and perception takes precedence. The result is a body of work that reads with unusual calm, depth, and interior gravity in private settings as well as curated displays.

Collections

Works are held in private collections across Europe. The estate now presents a selected body from the archive through a controlled release model.
