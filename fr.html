About
Alexey Listopad (1950–2020) was a painter, scenographer, and film artist whose practice evolved between Baltic landscapes, figurative compositions, and cinematic space.
Educated at the Penza Art College named after K.A. Savitsky, he later worked between Klaipeda and Moscow, participating in Soviet and post-Soviet exhibition contexts and developing a painterly language shaped by atmosphere, structure, and narrative.

His works are held in museum and private collections across Europe, the United States, and Asia.

Selected Biography

1950 — Born in Nyandoma, Arkhangelsk Region
1970–1974 — Penza Art College named after K.A. Savitsky
1975–1979 — Teaching and exhibition activity in Klaipeda
1980 — Relocation to Moscow
1981 — Moscow Union of Artists (Youth Association)
1989–1994 — Production designer in film
2005 — Member of the Union of Artists of Russia
Exhibitions
